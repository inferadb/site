Your questions, answered

What is a vault?

A vault is an isolated authorization namespace — a self-contained unit with its own relationships, policies, and audit trail. Think of it like a database in a database server. Each vault is cryptographically isolated on Scale plans.

What counts as a check?

A check is a single authorization query: "can user X perform action Y on resource Z?" Each API call to the Check endpoint counts as one check, regardless of complexity. Batch checks count as one check per item in the batch.

What counts as a relationship?

A relationship is a stored tuple that describes a connection between entities — for example, "user:alice is an editor of document:report". Relationships are counted by the total number stored, not by writes. Deleting a relationship frees the count.

What happens when I hit my free tier limits?

Your app keeps working. Free tier usage degrades gracefully with increased latency rather than hard-cutting requests. You'll receive email notifications as you approach your limits so you can upgrade before it affects your users.

How does overage billing work?

Paid plans include a base allocation of checks and relationships. Usage beyond that is billed monthly at the per-unit rate for your plan. There are no surprise charges — overage is metered and visible in your dashboard in real time.

Can I change plans at any time?

Yes. Upgrades take effect immediately. Downgrades take effect at the start of your next billing cycle. There are no long-term contracts on any self-serve plan.

What is the audit trail?

The audit trail is a tamper-evident log of every authorization decision InferaDB makes. On Scale plans, each entry is anchored to a Merkle tree for cryptographic proof that the record hasn't been altered.

Do you offer credits for startups?

Yes. Early-stage startups can apply for up to $10K in InferaDB Cloud credits through our Startup Program. We also offer free Scale-tier access for qualifying open source projects and educational institutions.

How does InferaDB compare to self-hosting OpenFGA or SpiceDB?

InferaDB Cloud eliminates the ops burden of running, scaling, and monitoring an authorization service. Beyond managed infrastructure, InferaDB offers features not available in open source alternatives: cryptographic tenant isolation, Merkle proof audit trails, the IPL policy language, and a visual dashboard. The free tier lets you evaluate without commitment.
