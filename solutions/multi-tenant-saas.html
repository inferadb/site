SEC.01 The Problem

Multi-tenant authorization is deceptively hard

Every SaaS product starts with simple roles: admin, editor, viewer. Then customers want per-team permissions. Then nested organizations. Then custom roles they define themselves. Then sharing links. The role matrix explodes, and suddenly your authorization layer is the most fragile part of your system.

Role explosion

N customers times M roles times P resources. Traditional RBAC collapses under combinatorial complexity. You end up with thousands of roles that nobody can reason about.

Cross-tenant data leaks

Application-level tenant isolation is one missed WHERE clause away from a breach. A single query bug exposes Customer A's data to Customer B.

N+1 permission queries

Listing "all documents this user can see" turns into hundreds of database queries. Search results become painfully slow or dangerously inaccurate.

SEC.02 The Solution

Relationship-based access control, built into the database

InferaDB replaces brittle role matrices with a relationship graph. Define entities, relations, and computed permissions once — the engine resolves arbitrarily deep hierarchies in microseconds.

ReBAC modeling

Express "user is member of team, team belongs to org, org owns workspace" as first-class relationships. Permissions compose through the graph — no role explosion.

Cryptographic vault isolation

Each tenant's authorization data lives in a cryptographically isolated vault. Cross-tenant leaks are architecturally impossible, not just unlikely.

Permission-aware search

Ask "which documents can user X view?" and get results in milliseconds. No N+1 queries, no post-filtering, no stale caches.
